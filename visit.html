Budapest is the capital and largest city of Hungary, and it is the political, economic, and industrial center of the country, especially after it became one city located on both banks of the Danube River in 1873, where it is inhabited by approximately 1.8 million people and is visited annually by approximately 30 million tourists for its beauty of nature and quiet life, so Budapest remains one of the jewels of European cities, its beauty and memories affect the psyche of the visitor, which makes tourism in Hungary and specifically tourism in Budapest as a scene stuck in the mind that is difficult for the tourist to forget over the years and while Below is a detailed and comprehensive report on life in Budapest.

1- Buda Castle

Buda Castle is one of the most famous tourist places in Hungary. It is one of the historical castles that are among the most famous landmarks of Budapest and is also part of the World Heritage site in Budapest. It was the residence of state kings, and its construction was completed in 1265 AD. It is bounded on the north by what is known as the Castle District or Farnegade, famous for its medieval houses, churches, buildings, and Baroque, 19th-century architecture. The hill is linked to Adam Clark Square and the Chain Bridge by the Castle Hill Railway.

Henrique Ferreira - Unplash

2- Hungarian Parliament Building

The Hungarian Parliament Building, located in Budapest, is one of the most important tourist attractions in Hungary. It is a wonderful example of neo-gothic architecture, although it includes many characteristics of the Baroque and Renaissance arts, and is more than 110 years old. Located opposite the Museum of Ethnography and the Hungarian Ministry of Agriculture, it ranks as the third largest parliament building in the world, with 691 rooms, 20 kilometers of stairs, and 96 meters high, the same height as St. Stephen's Church.

3- St. Stephen's Basilica

St. Stephen's Cathedral is the Roman Catholic cathedral in Budapest. In honor of Stephen, the first king of Hungary (975-1038). This is the sixth largest church in Hungary before 1920. Today it is the third largest church at present and the most important church in Hungary, one of the most important tourist attractions and the third highest church in Hungary.

4- Hungarian State Opera House

Considered the best Hungarian architectural achievement, the modern structure of the Renaissance is adorned with paintings and sculptures created by the country's artists, and its colossal richness is made of bronze, and the stage has attracted world-renowned composers, including Gustav Mahler and Otto Klemperer.

5- Matthias Church

Matthias Church is considered one of the finest churches in Budapest. The Matthias Church is one of the very unique churches in Europe. It is located on top of the Buda Hill Palace and has been used to serve the citizens of Buda Castle since 1015.
